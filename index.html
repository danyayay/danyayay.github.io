Danya Li

I am a Ph.D. student at the Intelligent Transport Systems (ITS) group at Technical University of Denmark (DTU), advised by Prof. Rico Krueger and Prof. Francisco C Pereira. My research focuses on machine learning for human behavior prediction in the context of autonomous driving and human-centric AI.

My work spans virtual reality (VR)-based user studies, trajectory prediction, multimodal sensor fusion, and vision-language models — with the goal of making autonomous systems safer and more human-aware.

I obtained my M.Sc. from EPFL (Civil Engineering, minor in Data Science) at Switzerland and my B.Eng. from Tongji University (Transportation Engineering) at China. I was also a visiting researcher at TU Delft, Netherlands.

Eye Gaze-Informed and Context-Aware Pedestrian Trajectory Prediction in Shared Spaces with Automated Shuttles: A Virtual Reality Study

Danya Li, Yan Feng, Rico Krueger

Under review, 2025

🗺️👀🚶 How much does a pedestrian's attention and context reveal about their next step?
By building a multimodal fusion network based on a VR study, we demonstrate that combining fine-grained visual attention with situational context provides a super-additive, highly accurate model for predicting human behavior.

Understanding and Predicting Human Behavior in Interactions with Autonomous Systems in Urban Environments: A Systematic Review, Challenges, and Opportunities

Danya Li, Wencan Mao, Francisco Pereira, Yu Xiao, Xiang Su, Rico Krueger

Transportation Research Part F: Traffic Psychology and Behaviour, 2025

🚴‍♂️🚶‍♀️🚗 🚌 How will pedestrians and self-driving cars safely share the streets of tomorrow?
Synthesizing 99 studies, this review exposes the blind spots in current human-AV predictive models and data. We provide a clear roadmap leveraging controlled experiments to build safer, more adaptable autonomous systems.

TwoResNet: Two-Level Resolution Neural Network for Traffic Forecasting of Freeway Networks

Danya Li*, Semin Kwak*, Nikolas Geroliminis

IEEE Intelligent Transportation Systems Conference (ITSC), 2022  |  Extended version: Scientific Reports, 2024

🛣️🚙🔮 Why does a traffic jam 10 miles away suddenly ruin your commute?
To capture the long-range ripple effects of urban congestion, we introduce a two-level network merging big-picture regional trends with granular local correlations. This delivers more accurate and interpretable long-term forecasts—even when sensor data is noisy or incomplete.

Motion Style Transfer: Modular Low-Rank Adaptation for Deep Motion Forecasting

Parth Kothari, Danya Li, Yuejiang Liu, Alexandre Alahi

Conference on Robot Learning (CoRL), 2022

🌍⚡🚦 Can we efficiently adapt pretrained motion models to unseen domains?
By decoupling universal motion dynamics from local behaviors, we built MoSA: a low-rank adapter that learns the unique "motion styles" of unseen agents and scenes at minimal cost, eliminating the need to retrain from scratch.
