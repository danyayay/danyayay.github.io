About ME

I am a Ph.D. researcher at the Machine Learning for Smart Mobility (MLSM) group at DTU, advised by Prof. Rico Krueger and Prof. Francisco C Pereira. My research focuses on machine learning for human behavior prediction in the context of autonomous driving and human-centric AI.

My work spans trajectory prediction, VR-based user studies, multimodal sensor fusion, and vision-language models — with the goal of making autonomous systems safer and more human-aware.

I obtained my M.Sc. from EPFL (Civil Engineering, minor in Data Science) and my B.Eng. from Tongji University (Transportation Engineering). I was also a visiting researcher at TU Delft.

Research Experience

Eye Gaze-Informed and Context-Aware Pedestrian Trajectory Prediction

MLSM, DTU · 2024 – 2025

Designed and built a high-fidelity VR simulation environment in Unreal Engine 5 to simulate critical pedestrian-AV interactions. Engineered an automated data pipeline to capture synchronized eye gaze, head pose, and trajectory data from human subjects. Proved that integrating physiological cues (gaze) and experimental contexts via a multimodal sensor fusion network reduces prediction error, providing key insights for driver monitoring and intention estimation systems.

Paper

Understanding and Predicting Human-AV Interactions: A Systematic Review

MLSM, DTU · 2023 – 2024

Systematically reviewed 99 articles and identified technical and methodological research gaps for human-AV interaction prediction systems. Evaluated existing work across five dimensions — scalability, criticality, versatility, adaptability, and generalizability — and proposed actionable directions for advancing the field.

Paper

Motion Style Transfer: Modular Low-Rank Adaptation for Deep Motion Forecasting

Master Thesis · VITA Lab, EPFL · 2022

Efficiently adapted pre-trained motion forecasting models to new domains with limited samples by adding low-rank motion style adapters, outperforming fine-tuning while updating only <5% extra parameters. A modular adapter strategy disentangles features of scene context and motion history, enabling fine-grained choice of adaptation layers given a-priori knowledge about the target domain.

Paper Code

TwoResNet: Two-Level Resolution Neural Network for Traffic Forecasting

Semester Project · LUTS, EPFL · 2020 – 2021

Proposed a two-level resolution deep neural network architecture that decomposes spatio-temporal correlations into low and high-resolution blocks, explaining general changes and fine details of traffic speed respectively. Applied to two real-world freeway datasets (METR-LA, PEMS-BAY) and obtained improved performance, especially for long-term prediction horizons. Extended to a journal version with enhanced interpretability analysis.

Paper (ITSC) Paper (Sci. Reports) Code

ML for Cropland Detection using Satellite Images

Data Scientist Intern · Empa · 2021 – 2022

Built a machine learning pipeline for cropland detection from satellite image time series in the Indian Himalayan region, generating an open-access cropland map to support agricultural monitoring and food security analysis.
